## 1. Corrected diagnosis

### What the new facts change

**The 06-10 strategy was built on one assumption that is now false — that coverage is the product
and money buys it.** "Panel everything, route the tail" (~$2–4k) is dead at $20: even the cheapest
reader, ds-flash at 1 rep, costs ~$27 corpus-wide. No amount of roster cleverness fixes that; the
architecture has to change, not the shopping list.

**The deeper correction is a reframe the old strategy missed entirely.** `lineation_decisions` is
not a new model competing with the panel — it is *the production importer's own verdict*, read back
out per source ordinal. The converter already labels every votable line in the corpus,
deterministically, for free, at 0.97 balanced-acc on gold and with a one-sided error profile. So
the task is no longer *"produce a label for every line"* — coverage exists by construction. The
task is **"find and fix the importer's mistakes"**: every LLM dollar must buy *error discovery*,
not label coverage. Labels-as-data shrinks to a committed *correction set* (human/override labels
by LineId where the importer is wrong), plus converter-rule fixes where a miss class is
rule-expressible. That reframe collapses the budget requirement by ~100× and it is what makes $20
sufficient rather than merely survivable.

**Acquisition is resurrected — with a better signal.** The old strategy demoted acquisition
("coverage, not selection") and killed student-uncertainty as a router. Both moves were right then;
the first is now wrong (selection is everything at $20) and the second has a successor: we now have
**two independent free corpus-wide views of every line** — the deterministic verdict (rule-based,
structural) and the student posterior (learned, φ) — and their *disagreement* is exactly the
two-views-of-one-source error detector that LO/OO promised and student-margin failed to be. It
costs $0 and exists today.

## 2. The $20 architecture — a one-way ratchet of escalating cost

The system is a ladder. Every line starts free; money is spent only where free signals disagree,
and human minutes only where paid signals disagree. The classifier's verified asymmetry is the
load-bearing beam.

```
tier 0  (free, 100% of lines)   importer verdict via lineation_decisions
        det=lineated  → ACCEPT lineated. Measured FP: 1/261 across all gold.
                        Audited only via the E1 random sample, never paneled.
        det=prose     → almost surely right (bulk of corpus), but ALL error mass lives here.
        uncovered/REVIEW (no span, mixed kinds) → auto-suspect, into tier 2.

tier 1  (free, 100% of lines)   suspicion ranking on the det=prose population
        signals: student posterior vs det verdict (the two-view disagreement),
        φ near-miss geometry (short-line runs, ragged right edge, centered, left-aligned
        unjustified — the justified⇒prose probe inverted), det evidence margins,
        REVIEW verdicts. E2 picks the winning combination on unbiased data.

tier 2  (~$0.06–0.17/1k)        ds-flash on the suspect slice, rep-adaptive
        1 rep base; escalate to 3 reps only on disagreement-with-det or per-rep conf < 0.7
        (the floor un-inerts at 1 rep — that is a feature here, it is the escalation trigger).
        agrees prose with det → ACCEPT prose.
        says lineated (disagrees with det) → tier 3.

tier 3  (~$0.5/1k of conflicts) gemini-lite on tier-2 conflicts
        agrees lineated → a DISCOVERED IMPORTER ERROR → tier 4.   split → tier 4 as "contested".

tier 4  (human, region-priced)  the studio, region-tiled
        discovered errors cluster in RUNS (a missed verse block is missed wholesale), so one
        human verdict prices a region, not a line. grok-vision reserve (≤$2) only as a second
        anchor opinion on the gnarliest contested regions before humans see them.

feedback (the multiplier)       every confirmed error class goes FIRST to RCA against the
        deterministic ladder: if rule-expressible, fix the converter (behind the gold-set
        regression below) and re-run tier 0 corpus-wide for free; only the residue becomes
        committed correction labels. $20 of discovery buys training signal for the RULES.
```

Three design decisions worth making explicit:

1. **The deterministic classifier joins the gate as a free voter.** Emit its verdicts as synthetic
   `PanelVote` rows (tag `ir-det`, verdict-dependent confidence calibrated on E1) so
   `policy_replay` and `route` treat it natively as a roster member. Classifier-vs-panel
   disagreement then falls out of the existing decision machinery instead of bespoke glue — and
   the policy sweep can optimize over rosters that include it. (This is votes-level integration;
   it does not touch the completer abstraction.)
2. **The gold-set regression becomes a committed check.** The 4-set scoring of
   `lineation_decisions` (the table at the top) is the regression harness for every converter-rule
   change the feedback loop proposes. Converter changes are production `pancratius/` work
   (CLAUDE.md boundaries apply: tests, `npm run verify`, goldens) — the lesson from the rejected
   `<w:br>` auto-rule is *unverified* rules are banned, not rules.
3. **Reps are bought, not defaulted.** E1's 3-rep votes let every cheaper protocol (1-rep,
   rep-adaptive, roster subsets) be simulated by downsampled replay for $0 before a dollar of E3
   is committed.

### Resolving the inside-φ vs outside-φ fork (designed, not vibed)

The anti-correlation result came from a student trained on enriched, 51%-saturated gold. The fork:
panel ambiguity lives *inside* φ (→ representative training fixes the sign and student-uncertainty
becomes a free acquisition signal for later rounds) vs *outside* φ (→ no φ-based AL; the router
stays two-view disagreement). Resolution: retrain the student on E1's representative working half,
re-measure Spearman(margin, panel-disagreement) and the tercile off-diagonals under book-held-out
CV *within that half* (never touching the frozen acceptance half). Pre-registered criterion:
**ρ ≥ +0.3 with monotone terciles → inside-φ**; anything weaker → outside-φ, fork closed, new
features become an audit-only concern. Either answer is useful; neither costs anything.

## 3. The experiments — critical path

Prices use the verified scorecard $/1k (3-rep unless stated). Human time is the binding secondary
budget; it is priced per item.

**E0 — corpus reconnaissance ($0, ~half-day compute). RUN FIRST.**
Land/commit the in-flight IR fix; build records for all 103 docx; run `lineation_decisions` +
student posterior corpus-wide. Outputs: true votable N (assumed ~450–500k — *measure it*),
per-book det lineated-% (the book-prior guardrail), the det-vs-student disagreement map, suspicion
ranking v0, the uncovered/REVIEW fate ledger, and the EN structural sanity scan (free first look at
all 29 en books). *Resolves:* the denominator; the size of the suspect slice (sizes E3); whether EN
looks structurally alien. *Success:* every votable line has a tier-0 verdict or a ledger entry.

**E0b — post-fix regression ($0, minutes).**
Re-run the 4-set deterministic scoring after the IR fix. *Resolves:* how much error mass the free
fix already removed (contested lin-recall from 0.812, structural from 0.625). *Success:* hard
slices improve, prose-recall stays ≥0.988, trainable-gold does not regress. Commit the script as
the standing regression (it is no longer a one-off — it gates every feedback-loop rule change).

**E1 — the keystone instrument (~$6.3 + ~3–4 h human).**
1,500 votable lines, *uniformly random* (region-tiled for human/vision efficiency), stratified
only proportionally: book × language (~400 en) × tier-0 verdict. Full production panel, 3 reps,
frozen contracts. Route per the calibrated gate (~5–10% expected on random data → 75–150 lines to
the studio); human spot-check ~300 accepted lines *prioritizing every det-vs-gate disagreement*.
Then split 750/750: **frozen acceptance half (never trained, never tuned on, scored once in E4)**
and working half (AL round 1, bakeoff substrate, student retraining). *Resolves:* corpus base
rate; **P(truth=lineated | det=prose) — THE number that sizes the residual risk**; gate
accept-accuracy on representative (not enriched) data; EN parity; real routing rate; det-voter
confidence calibration. *Success:* the instrument exists; nothing else in the plan is gated on a
particular value — the values steer E3's size and the DoD thresholds.

**E2 — signal bakeoff + policy shopping ($0, replay on E1 working half).**
(a) Rank the free signals (det-vs-student disagreement, student margin, suspicion-v0 features,
panel-conf C as ceiling) as detectors of det errors and of route-worthy lines; pick the corpus
router by recall-at-budget on the E3 spend curve. (b) Run the fork-resolution protocol above.
(c) Downsample-replay rep protocols and rosters (with and without the `ir-det` voter) and pick the
cheapest policy meeting the accept-accuracy bar. *Resolves:* the router, the fork, the E3 recipe.
*Success:* a chosen router whose recall of E1's discovered det-errors at E3's budget beats random
sampling by ≥3× (else E3 falls back to uniform sampling of det=prose and the budget buys a bound
instead of a sweep — still a valid endgame, just weaker).

**E3 — the targeted error sweep (~$8–10 + human tail through the week).**
Rank all corpus det=prose lines (plus all REVIEW/uncovered) by the E2 router; walk the ladder:
ds-flash rep-adaptive (≈$0.06–0.17/1k ⇒ $8 covers ~50–130k lines depending on escalation rate) →
gemini-lite on conflicts → grok-vision reserve ≤$2 on the worst contested regions → studio,
region-tiled, worked weekly never piled. Every confirmed error class → converter RCA first
(behind E0b regression), label residue second. Re-run tier 0/1 maps after each converter fix —
the suspect slice *shrinks as you spend*. *Resolves:* the error mass itself. *Success:* the
router's discovery rate decays to near-flat (marginal $ buys no new error regions) OR budget
exhausted with the residual bounded by E4.

**E4 — acceptance scoring ($0, once).**
Score the *final combined system* (importer + corrections) against the frozen 750. Wilson bounds,
en stratum reported separately, calibration by tier. This is the single use of the frozen half.

**E5 — fork follow-through ($0, optional).**
If E2 said inside-φ: retrain on E1+E3 labels, verify student-uncertainty now routes (on E3
discoveries, not the frozen set), and adopt it as the free router for any future round/new books.
If outside-φ: write the negative result and stop — the two-view router remains the standing audit.

Critical path: **E0 → E0b → E1 → E2 → E3 → E4.** E5 rides along. Total spend ≈ $16–18 with ~$2–3
slack; human ≈ 1–1.5 days spread across the week.

Pruned as not decision-relevant under $20: any further reader/prompt/temp work, the LO/OO bakeoff
(still), qwen anything, the acquire-300 selection (superseded — E1's random working half IS AL
round 1), the standalone $30 dress rehearsal (paid), full-corpus paneling in any roster.

## 4. Definition of done, re-derived under $20

Done when:

1. **Coverage by construction.** Every votable line of 74 ru + 29 en books has a tier-0 importer
   verdict; uncovered/REVIEW ordinals enumerated in the committed fate ledger (<0.1% or explicitly
   excepted). The committed artifact is the *correction set* (human/override labels by LineId,
   hash-railed) + the ledger — not a per-line label dump.
2. **Measured accuracy on the frozen instrument (E4, scored once).**
   - Combined-system accuracy ≥ 98% Wilson 95% lower bound on the frozen 750;
   - asymmetric bounds set from E1's measured base rate — the binding one is residual
     **lineated→prose ≤ 2% of the lineated stratum** (the importer's weak direction);
     prose→lineated ≈ 0 (the free direction; verify it stayed free);
   - EN stratum meets the same bars separately, never averaged in.
3. **The error ledger is closed.** Every E3-discovered error region adjudicated and fixed — by
   converter rule (preferred, regression-gated) or committed correction label; the studio queue
   empty.
4. **Wiring.** The converter consumes correction labels by LineId behind hash rails; md
   regenerated for corrected books; `npm run verify` green; the deterministic-regression check
   committed.
5. **Reproducibility.** Every paid call replayable $0 from caches; every correction traces to a
   manifest (prompt, contract, roster incl. `ir-det`, policy, prices, git SHA).

The acceptance instrument: 1,500 random region-tiled lines, proportional book × language ×
tier-0-verdict strata; 750 frozen (acceptance only, single use) / 750 working (AL round 1, bakeoff,
student retraining). One sample, one panel run, three jobs — instrument, acquisition seed, and
unbiased substrate — which is what makes $6.3 the best-spent third of the budget.

## 5. The EN bootstrap (29 books, 0 labels today)

Cheapest path, in order, all inside the plan above rather than a separate track:
1. **E0 free scan** — det verdicts + per-book lineated-% + φ distributions for all 29; flag any
   book whose structural profile (justification, indent, short-line geometry) falls outside the ru
   envelope.
2. **E1 en stratum (~400 lines, ≈$1.7 of the E1 budget)** — full panel + human adjudication gives
   the first en truth, measures det error rate and panel parity on en in one shot.
3. **Decision rule:** en-vs-ru det error profiles comparable (within ~2 pts, same direction) →
   EN folds into the E3 ranking with no special treatment, done. Profiles diverge → the divergence
   is structural and visible in φ; build an en-specific suspect slice from the E0 scan (still tier
   1, still free) and spend a ds-flash tranche there. The prompts are structural and
   language-agnostic; do not author en prompts unless E1 shows a panel (not classifier) gap.

## 6. Register — what carries over

The harness, the studio, the votes/route/ingest loop, the manifest discipline, and now the
*budget* discipline: the E1 pattern (one random region-tiled instrument that triples as acceptance
set, acquisition seed, and signal substrate) is the reusable shape, and R0 from the 06-10 strategy
(~120–150 lineated runs, two human passes + mini-panel, read κ / transfer / variance decomposition)
survives unchanged and fits the same ~$5-grade envelope. What does NOT carry: the deterministic
classifier (register is linguistic, not structural — there is no free tier-0 labeler, so register's
economics are genuinely different and its population, lineated runs, only exists after E3 anyway)
and φ (the student needs lexical features or stays LLM-only). Register starts the day E3's
corrected lineation surface exists for a dozen books.

## 7. Bottom line

The budget collapse is not a setback to route around — it exposed that the expensive plan was
solving the wrong problem. The corpus does not need 500k purchased labels; it has a free labeler
with a verified one-sided error profile, a free learned second opinion, a calibrated gate, and a
trained human workflow. $20 is not a constraint on the old plan; it is roughly the correct price
of the new one: ~$6 to mint the instrument that calibrates the free labeler and seeds active
learning, ~$10 of cheap-reader spend routed by free two-view disagreement into the one direction
the labeler fails, ~$2 of anchor reserve, and a feedback loop that converts discovered errors into
converter rules so the free tier improves while you spend. Run E0 today; E1 is the only purchase
that must happen before anything else is decided.
