LINEATED, read as a BLOCK: a run of short lines that stop early and stack together; a sentence
deliberately split across lines; parallel members after a colon or dash; a contrast across lines
("Не с неба — / а из сердца"); a list or litany; broken vows / prayers / invocations; runs set off by
stanza gaps. This holds even when the words would read fine as ordinary narrative or dialogue — what
matters is the block's shape on the page.

PROSE, read as a BLOCK: full sentences that WRAP and run on as one flowing paragraph, even when a
sentence is short or carries a speaker label ("Панкратиус: мы привыкли смотреть на вещи…"); complete
sentences laid one per line, even with anaphora ("Ты… / Ты…"), are a paragraph, not a litany. When
the block reads true as a paragraph and nothing is lost, it is prose.
