UNIT BEFORE LINE. Read consecutive lines as the display unit they form — a prose paragraph, a
numbered/titled item, a list, a litany, a dialogue turn — and label by what the unit is doing. For a
numbered/titled item ("1. Title" then its text as one block), the title/body break is intentional
lineation even though the body wraps.

STRUCTURAL CUES you CAN read without a page:
  toward LINEATED — a run of consecutive SHORT nowrap lines (the single strongest cue: they stop early
    by choice, prose would have filled them); a sentence split across lines (one line ends mid-thought,
    the next completes it, lowercase start); short parallel members after a colon or dash; a short
    standalone title / lead-in / speaker turn ("Светозар сказал:").
  toward PROSE — a line flagged WRAPS that is a complete flowing sentence (wrapping is prose evidence);
    several full sentences in a row, even with anaphora ("Ты… / Ты…") or a speaker label ("Панкратиус:
    мы привыкли смотреть…"), reading as one paragraph.

PROSE GUARDRAIL — do NOT over-lineate: a WRAPPING full sentence is prose even if short or
speaker-labeled; choose prose whenever the break does no visible reader-facing work. Because you have
no page, lean on the CONTENT cues above (nowrap runs, sentence completeness, colon/dash members) rather
than on exact block grouping, which the listing may have mis-extracted. Hard markers (heading / *** /
image / blank / right-aligned / blockquote) separate units and are never labelled.
