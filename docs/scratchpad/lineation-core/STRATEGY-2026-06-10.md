# Lineation → done, then register — strategy (2026-06-10)

Principal-architect read of the actual tree, data, and experiment artifacts. Everything below was
verified against the repo on this date, not relayed: 257 tests green; student CV re-run and
reproduced (balAcc 0.961, prose P/R 0.876/0.944, smoothing flat at α=0.75); `build → panel → route →
ingest` confirmed wired end-to-end in `teacher/recipes.py` (task-stamped votes, precedence-safe,
refuse-on-partial); label inventory 622 human labels / 26 books / **ru only**; votes.jsonl = 3,161
legacy-panel rows (tags gemini/deepseek/grok/owl/minimax/mimo, no `task` stamp — cannot drive
`route`); `_artifacts` = 33 ru books, 217,337 lines, 205,160 votable, 4,797 unmapped + 2,002 mixed;
corpus on disk = **74 ru + 29 en DOCX** (103 files); the live prompts already carry UNIT-BEFORE-LINE
+ the prose guardrail; the 2026-06-10 bench artifacts (contract-roster, temps, tsv) all present with
manifests.

## 1. Diagnosis — where we actually are

### Genuinely decided (stop researching these)
- **The substrate.** One producer, one LineRecord, LineId + hash rails, store as the single disk
  boundary, parity between teacher listing and student vector. Proven by tests; this is the asset
  that makes everything else cheap. Done.
- **The instrument.** The eval harness (TOML experiments, per-reader scorecards, provenance
  manifests, $0 replay, fail-loud pricing) is real and already caught the things single runs lied
  about. Done.
- **Reader panel shape.** grok (lead, vision-native) / gemini-lite / ds-flash; qwen-235b parked,
  qwen-flash rejected (key-fidelity disaster: 1,233–1,535 unmapped_key), TSV negative
  (coverage 0.748 on the cleanest reader). Per-(reader,modality) contract interaction is REAL:
  gemini-vision collapses under keyed (prose recall 0.989 → 0.589) while every text reader is clean
  or better under keyed. 3-rep aggregation at temp 0.5 is the protocol. Decided — freeze it (§3 P0).
- **Decision policy CLASS.** Anchor-led with conf floor beats unanimous (same balAcc 0.969, 4.5×
  less human load) and equal-majority (under-lineates). The mechanism and its TOML grammar are
  built, tested, and live in `route`. Decided as a mechanism.
- **Student-uncertainty-as-acquisition is dead.** The anti-correlation result (ρ −0.13 raw, −0.29
  at deployed α, wrong-signed, ~30% mis-routed at the extremes) is solid — replicated against the
  obvious saturation objection. Do not re-tread.
- **Panel confidence C is the within-panel ambiguity signal.** ρ = −0.50 vs disagreement, monotone,
  non-degenerate. This is the validated triage signal and it comes FREE with every paneled line.

### Soft (claimed or implied, not yet evidence-grade)
1. **The policy THRESHOLDS, not the policy class.** `min_core_agree=2, conf_floor=0.7` was settled
   by replaying the **legacy six-reader panel's votes** under the old prompt/protocol. The new
   roster's confidence distributions are different animals (legacy C_mean clumped 0.78–0.97). No
   vote from the new roster has ever passed through the gate. The "settled" policy is settled in
   shape only — its numbers are uncalibrated for the thing we will actually run.
2. **The bilingual claim.** Zero en labels, zero en eval rows, zero en records built. Features are
   language-agnostic by construction and fixture tests, but no evidence the en books share the
   Enter-every-line pathology or that the panel performs on en. A third of the corpus (29 books) is
   currently aspiration.
3. **Corpus-wide rates.** Every number we have (prose/lineated 1:6, human-route 6.4%, panel
   disagreement bins) comes from ENRICHED samples (contested lines, stratified benches). The two
   numbers that size the endgame — corpus base rate and corpus human-routing rate — are unknown.
4. **Single-run deltas inside the noise floor.** ds-flash 0.5-vs-0.0 (+5.0 pts, n=270) is
   directionally fine but ~2× the noise floor at best. Freeze it and move on; do not cite it as a
   precision fact.
5. **α=0.75 smoothing.** On the current 620-label set it changes nothing (balAcc 0.961 at α=0 and
   α=0.75; 16 flips). Keep it as a composable layer; stop claiming it as a gain.

### Blocking a shippable pipeline (the actual gaps)
- **Producer debt: the §14-P1 span-drop.** 4,797 unmapped + 2,002 mixed lines in just 33 books
  (~3%; ~12–15k corpus-wide projected). Unmapped lines are `votable=False` — they can NEVER receive
  a label, so the corpus run would silently ship holes. This is the single highest-leverage fix and
  it is pure engineering, no LLM.
- **No new-roster votes through the gate.** (Soft #1 above — it blocks the live run.)
- **The escalation wrapper** (adaptive reps on OPERATIONAL routing reasons) — deliberately
  deferred, but required at corpus scale; `route` already surfaces the seam it acts on.
- **Production consumption.** Nothing in `pancratius/` consumes labels. The converter still runs
  the heuristic Q1 lineation inference (`ir/normalize.py` §11). The graduation seam is named in
  SPEC but undesigned.
- **No acceptance instrument.** There is no frozen, never-trained, corpus-representative,
  human-verified sample against which "done" could even be scored. Every existing eval set is
  enriched and historically entangled with the panel.

### Where I challenge the framing
1. **"The decision policy is UNWIRED" — no longer true.** `recipes route` + `ingest --task-id` is
   built, tested (`test_route.py`), precedence-safe, task-scoped. What is genuinely unwired is (a)
   the escalation wrapper and (b) the converter/site side. Don't re-do what exists; finish the two
   real gaps.
2. **"Acquisition strategy" is the wrong frame now.** Acquisition (choosing which lines to label)
   matters when labels are scarce. Money is not binding, the corpus is finite and static
   (~450–500k votable lines, ~$2.5–4k for the full 3-reader×3-rep panel at benched $/1k). The
   SOTA move is **panel everything, route the tail** — coverage, not selection. The
   student-isn't-a-proxy result then has a different consequence than the one in the brief: it
   doesn't break acquisition (which we no longer need), it **re-assigns the student from
   production labeler to independent auditor**. Student-vs-gate disagreement is a second error
   detector roughly orthogonal to panel-internal splits (structure-view vs semantics-view) — that
   orthogonality, which killed the proxy idea, is exactly what makes a good audit queue.
3. **The Phase-B LO/OO bakeoff should be PRUNED from the critical path.** Its purpose was a cheap
   corpus-wide proxy for panel disagreement, to decide where to spend panel calls. Under
   panel-everything there is nothing to target: every line gets the panel, and the validated
   C-signal (ρ −0.50) arrives free on every line for human-queue triage. Also note the proxy's
   prospects are poor on the evidence we already hold: the LO-vs-IR structural comparison found 345
   split events across 381k lines (344 of them blockquote representation quirks) — structural
   converter disagreement is orders of magnitude too sparse to track ~30%-scale semantic panel
   splits, and OnlyOffice headless tooling doesn't exist in-tree (only 3 legacy exports). Revive the
   bakeoff ONLY in the fallback world where the pilot kills panel-everything and targeting becomes
   necessary again. (Then it's a half-day read-only study against existing votes, same method as
   RESULTS-student-vs-panel.)
4. **The acquire-300 run should be CANCELLED, not run.** It selects by student uncertainty — the
   signal just proven anti-aligned with what the oracle should see — and its goal (improve the
   student with ~300 labels) is superseded: the corpus run will hand the student ~10³× more
   gate+human labels than any active-learning round. Keep `selections/acquire.json` as a diagnostic
   slice if you like; spend its budget on the pilot instead.
5. **"SOTA" needs deflating into something falsifiable.** There is no external leaderboard for
   this task. SOTA here = *human-grade labels at corpus scale with a defensible audit trail*: a
   measured accept-accuracy bound on a fresh random sample, a fully-adjudicated residual, and
   reproducible provenance. That is what §2 pins down.

## 2. Definition of done

Lineation is **done** when all six hold. After they hold, model/prompt/policy research STOPS; the
only standing process is the audit in P8.

1. **Coverage.** Every votable body line of all 74 ru + 29 en books carries a committed `LineLabel`
   with provenance ∈ {gate, human, override}. Unmapped/held lines < 0.1% of body lines, each
   enumerated in a fate ledger (explicit exceptions, never silence).
2. **Accuracy bar** — scored once against the FROZEN acceptance set (P3: ~1,200 random
   stratified lines incl. en, human-adjudicated, never trained or tuned on):
   - gate-accepted accuracy ≥ 99.0%, Wilson 95% lower bound ≥ 98.0%;
   - asymmetric class bounds: lineated→prose ≤ 1.0% (flattening authorial intent is the costlier
     error), prose→lineated ≤ 1.5%;
   - calibration: empirical accuracy by decision-reason × anchor-conf bin is monotone, no
     populated bin < 95%.
3. **Human tail.** Every routed line human-adjudicated through the studio; queue EMPTY at ship.
   Routing rate within the budget set by the pilot (target ≤ 2% of lines, region-tiled). If the
   measured rate exceeds budget, thresholds are re-derived through `policy_replay` on the frozen
   sets — never loosened ad hoc.
4. **Wiring.** The converter consumes committed labels by LineId behind hash rails (fail-loud on
   docx drift), the heuristic Q1 inference is retired for labeled books, all md regenerated, site
   verified (`npm run verify`), a label-coverage gate added to `audit:repo`, and a sampled visual
   page audit passes.
5. **Reproducibility.** The whole corpus run replays $0 from caches; every label traces to a
   manifest pinning prompt, contract, roster, policy, prices, git SHA.
6. **EN parity.** The en slice of the acceptance set meets the same bars (2) independently — not
   averaged away inside the corpus number.

## 3. The path — minimal ordered sequence

Costs assume benched prices (3 readers × 3 reps ≈ $4.9/1k lines: grok-vision 4.13 + gemini-lite
0.57 + ds-flash 0.22); the pilot re-derives the real $/line before the big spend.

**P0 — Freeze the bench (½ day, $0).** Write `RESULTS-2026-06-10.md`; commit the production panel
recipe: grok = vision + json_keyed @0.5; gemini-lite = text + json_keyed @0.5 (if it ever runs
vision, json_array — the keyed-vision collapse is its one landmine); ds-flash = text + json_keyed
@0.5; reps = 3, aggregate before deciding. *Settles: reader/contract/temp research is over.*

**P1 — Producer debt + full-corpus records (1–2 days, $0).** Fix the §14-P1 span ownership loss
(root: `merge_source_spans` dropping provenance through verse-merge; fix where ownership is lost,
behind tests, goldens byte-checked for unaffected books). Build records for all 103 docx (en
included). Output: true corpus N, votable counts, fate ledger, structural base-rate sanity per
book. *Settles: 100% addressable universe; the cost/scale denominator; the needs_review flag stops
being a data caveat.*

**P2 — Gate dress rehearsal (1 day, ~$30).** Run the frozen panel (P0) on `reader_bench` (270) +
`contested` (425). Replay anchor-led across a threshold sweep (conf_floor × min_core_agree) on the
NEW votes; inspect per-reader conf distributions; pick thresholds by accept-accuracy ≥ bar at
minimum human-load. *Settles: the policy thresholds on the roster that will actually run — kills
the "settled on the historical protocol" caveat, the last soft spot in the decision layer.*

**P3 — Corpus pilot = the keystone (2–3 days, ~$50 + ~1 day human).** Random stratified ~1,200
votable lines, proportional across ALL books and BOTH languages (~300 en). Run the full live loop:
build → panel → route → adjudicate (studio) → ingest. Additionally human-verify a ~400-line random
subsample of the ACCEPTED lines (not just the routed ones). Measures: corpus base rate;
human-routing rate; accept accuracy + Wilson bound; en parity; real $/line; human min/region
throughput. The adjudicated sample (minus anything used for threshold tuning) is FROZEN as the
acceptance set of §2. *Settles: feasibility of panel-everything; the acceptance instrument; en
viability. Decision gate: routing rate ≤ 3% and en accuracy within 2 pts of ru → GO. Fallback
branch: en-specific prompt fix and/or threshold re-derivation; only if coverage is truly infeasible
does targeting (and with it the LO/OO bakeoff) come back.*

**P4 — The two missing operational pieces (parallel with P3, 1–2 days, $0).**
(a) The adaptive-reps escalation wrapper: OPERATIONAL reasons (anchor abstain, thin coverage) →
auto re-run/escalate to ~5 reps; TERMINAL reasons → human, as designed. (b) `ingest` auto-retires
consumed adjudication bundles (the deferred Codex finding) — at 100+ tasks the manual clearing
becomes an error source. *Settles: the loop runs unattended at scale.*

**P5 — Wave 1 vertical slice (3–5 diverse books, ~$100, 2–3 days).** Not just labels: through the
CONVERTER and the SITE. Design the graduation seam here — the converter consumes committed labels
as DATA by LineId behind hash rails (labels-as-data, not student-in-serve-path; the model never
sits in production). Regenerate those books' md, render, visual audit. *Settles: the end-to-end
product on real books before the mass spend; the pancratius boundary design (contract change —
classify against `docs/content-model.md`/`docs/architecture.md` and propose explicitly, per
CLAUDE.md).*

**P6 — Corpus waves (~1–2 weeks calendar, ~$2–4k total).** Remaining books in waves of ~10:
panel → route → human queue worked PER WAVE (never piled to the end). Per-wave QA dashboard:
accept rate, base rate per book (the hierarchical book-prior as a GUARDRAIL — a book whose
lineated-% departs wildly from its own pilot sample gets audited, never auto-corrected), reason
histogram, cost. Retrain the student each wave on accumulated gate+human labels; sample the
student-vs-gate disagreement queue into the human audit. *Settles: the labels.*

**P7 — Acceptance + ship.** Score §2 against the frozen set. Any failed bin → diagnose, re-derive
thresholds via replay, re-route affected lines; never hand-edit. Then: regenerate all md, retire
heuristic Q1 for labeled books, `npm run verify`, audit gates, evict `intent-classifier/`
(extraction already concluded — nothing left to pull), graduate the artifact contract + physics
into `pancratius/` per the SPEC seam. *Settles: shipped.*

**P8 — Standing audit (post-done, ~zero cost).** New/changed docx fail loud on hash rails →
producer → student scores it → panel only on student-flagged or new material; drift monitor on
reader behavior. Research stays closed unless the audit reopens it with evidence.

Pruned as research-for-its-own-sake: the Phase-B bakeoff (conditional fallback only), the
acquire-300 run, any further prompt A/B (the live prompts already embed the validated
UNIT-BEFORE-LINE + guardrail; the production gate, not prompt micro-deltas, is now the quality
lever), TSV resurrection, qwen re-qualification, CRF revisits.

| # | Risk | Retire by |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Corpus human-routing rate ≫ 2% → human tail infeasible | P3 measures it on a random sample before any commitment; fallbacks ordered: region-tiling density ↑ → threshold re-derivation under the accuracy bar → C-signal-prioritized partial adjudication with flagged-accept residual (explicit, audited) |
| 2 | Policy thresholds don't transfer to new-roster conf distributions | P2 dress rehearsal — $30, before anything live |
| 3 | EN books differ structurally (different authoring habit) or panel underperforms on en | P1 structural base-rate scan (free) + P3 en stratum; en prompt variant if needed |
| 4 | Span-ownership fix shifts LineIds/hashes and strands the 622 committed labels | src_ordinals come from the source and stay put; the fix is additive (previously-unmapped lines gain ownership). | 5 | Vision render fidelity confounds the lead reader (render = f(XML)) | already mitigated: page-only authored render via `pancratius.docx_render`, per-page images; P5's visual audit double-checks on real books |
| 6 | Single-run noise re-enters via per-wave spot decisions | protocol frozen at P0: 3-rep aggregation, CI on any go/no-go number, thresholds only via replay on frozen sets |
| 7 | Eval circularity (acceptance set entangled with panel history) | the P3 acceptance set is FRESH random lines adjudicated by humans under the studio, leave-one-out-clean by construction; existing enriched sets stay tuning-only |
| 8 | Concurrent-agent tree churn corrupting the long corpus run | runs are resumable ($0 replay), votes task-stamped, store fail-loud; run waves from a worktree; re-check HEAD before any history op |
| 9 | Human queue quality (fatigue, book-prior voting — seen in the orphan guardrail batches) | studio notes mandatory on low-confidence verdicts; per-wave 5% double-adjudication to measure human-human agreement; keep mixed-criterion batches out of truth (already policy) |

## 5. The register bridge

Lineation was partly the vehicle; here is precisely what the vehicle carries over.

**Transfers as-is (the expensive 80%).** The substrate (LineRecord/LineId, runs/regions, store,
hash rails); the teacher machinery (tasks/opaque keys, responses choke point, panel runner,
`route`, the studio — `lineOptions` is already generic, a register label set drops in); the eval
family (TOML experiments, scorecards, manifests, $0 replay, frozen eval-set discipline); the
decision-policy machinery (anchor-led shape, roster-as-config, replay harness); and — most
valuable — the PROTOCOL literacy: 3-rep aggregation, noise floor, native modality, leave-one-out
instrument validation, evidence-never-truth.

**Genuinely new (the hard 20%).**
- *The construct.* "Register" is not yet a measurable label. Minimum viable taxonomy per the
  verse-lineation plan: verse-voice vs lineated-prose, decided per lineated RUN — which makes
  register **downstream of lineation by construction** (its population = lineated runs). Lineation
  must ship first; this is a dependency, not a preference.
- *The unit.* Plausibly hierarchical book→region→line (domain hypothesis, NOT data-proven). The
  decision unit is probably the run/region; the studio needs its region-mode actually minted
  (small extension — the payload shape exists).
- *The panel.* Roleplay/persona readers HURT lineation but were noted to help register; modality
  likely flips text-dominant (register is linguistic, not visual — the LUPI page-render privilege
  mostly evaporates, and with it the render-fidelity confound). A fresh reader bench is required;
  do not port the lineation roster by inertia.
- *The features.* φ is structural geometry; an interpretable register student needs
  lexical/syntactic features or stays LLM-only longer. Do not assume the distillation step
  transfers on the same timeline.

**The smallest decisive experiment (R0 — construct validity, ~$10 + 1 human-day).** Sample
~120–150 lineated RUNS across ≥10 books from the (by then real) lineation labels. Two independent
human passes + adjudication → inter-human κ. In parallel, a 3-reader mini-panel (one
standard-instruction reader, one persona/roleplay reader, one cheap floor) on the same runs, native
text modality. Read three numbers: human-human κ (is the construct real? κ < ~0.6 → definition
work, no ML), panel-vs-human balAcc (does the teacher method transfer?), and a variance
decomposition of labels across book/region/line (does the hierarchical prior hold, and at which
level should the decision sit?). That single experiment tells you whether the lineation method
generalizes to register before a dollar of harness adaptation is spent — and its design costs
nothing because every instrument already exists.

**Sequencing.** R0 can run the moment P6's first waves yield trustworthy lineated runs for a dozen
books — it does NOT wait for P7. If R0's κ is healthy, register inherits a running harness, a
trained human workflow, and a frozen-protocol culture; the register-specific work reduces to a
construct doc, a prompt family, a reader bench, and its own acceptance set, on the same rails.

## 6. Bottom line

The research phase is over on the merits: substrate proven, instrument proven, panel and policy
class settled, the two seductive shortcuts (student-as-proxy, cheap structural oracle) properly
killed or demoted. What stands between here and done is not another model insight — it is one
producer bug, one threshold re-validation ($30), one keystone pilot ($50) that simultaneously
measures feasibility and mints the acceptance instrument, two small operational pieces, and a
disciplined ~$3k corpus run wired through the converter. Stop optimizing readers; start burning
down the corpus. Register then starts from a running harness and a one-day validity probe, not
from zero.
