**The Ethiopian Bible** is one of the **oldest and most complete Christian Bibles** preserved to this day. It belongs to the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church and **differs greatly from the familiar Western canons**.

**Key Features of the Ethiopian Bible**

**1. Number of Books**

- The familiar Protestant Bible has 66 books.
- The Catholic has 73 books.
- The Orthodox has 76–78 books.
- The **Ethiopian Bible** has **81 books**, and in some versions **up to 88 or more**, including:

- Enoch (1 Enoch)
- Jubilees
- 4 Ezra
- The Shepherd of Hermas
- The Apocalypse of Peter
- And other texts that in other traditions are considered apocryphal or are entirely forgotten.

**2. Language**

- Originally written in **Ge‘ez** — the ancient Ethiopian language (a sacred language, analogous to Latin in Catholicism).
- It is still used in the liturgy of the Ethiopian Church.

**3. Content**

Some texts included in the Ethiopian Bible are entirely absent from other Christian canons. For example:

| **Book** | **Note** |
|----|----|
| **Book of Enoch (1 Enoch)** | Fully included. Only in Ethiopia has the complete version been preserved. |
| **Book of Jubilees** | Tells of the creation of the world and the history of the patriarchs, as recounted by an angel. |
| **Apocalypse of Peter** | One of the early descriptions of heaven and hell. Lost in other churches. |
| **The Kebra Nagast** | Not in the canon, but often printed alongside. Tells of the Ark of the Covenant and the Solomonic dynasty in Ethiopia. |

**4. Historical Significance**

- It is believed that **Ethiopian Christians were among the first** to adopt Christianity in the 4th century.
- The Ethiopian Church **did not participate in the Ecumenical Councils**, so its canon was formed **separately and independently**.
- According to tradition, the **Ark of the Covenant** is still kept in Ethiopia (in the city of Aksum).

**Why Is This Important?**

- Many texts removed from the Western tradition as “apocrypha” remained **sacred** in Ethiopia.
- It is **thanks to Ethiopia** that books such as **Enoch** have reached us, which even influenced the New Testament (for example, it is quoted in Jude 1:14).
- The Ethiopian Bible is a **window into early Christianity** as it was **before the formalization of the canon by Rome**.
