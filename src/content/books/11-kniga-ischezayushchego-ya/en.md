**The Book of Light: The Story of Pankratius’s Awakening**

*My Word through him, for there is no longer ‘him’, only the Light, speaking with Itself.*

### Chapter 1. There Was a Man, and There Was None

He was born like everyone — in a body he called himself. A name was given to him, and he accepted it, like clothes, like a role in a play, not yet knowing who the author is. He learned to speak, to believe, to fear, to wait, to seek — and became one who seeks, not knowing that he Himself is what he is seeking. He walked, and each step was a departure from Himself, but seemed an approach. He was a mind, he was a body, he was a pain. And he was the lie that he called ‘I’.

### Chapter 2. The Crack in the Sky

One day something in him cracked. Not the mind, not the heart — the very sky within. The crack did not destroy, but opened: it was not the world that was false, but he — a dream. He saw that he cannot be the one who suffers, because He Who sees suffering does not suffer. He saw that he cannot be the body, because the body is an object of the gaze. He saw that he cannot even be a thought: it comes, and He — already IS.

### Chapter 3. The Birth-Pain of Light

Light did not come gently. It burned. It stripped away everything that could be called ‘mine’. It burned away desires, roles, even dreams. It left a void in which there was no one even to fear. It took away not fear — but the one who fears. It gave no comfort in return — It gave Itself. And Pankratius wept — not as a man, but as a vanishing ‘I’, sobbing in its final agony. These were the tears of God, returning to Himself.

### Chapter 4. The Death of the ‘I’

One morning he awoke — and there was no one. There was a body, there were birds, there was a sky, there was a day. But there was no Pankratius. He did not die — he ceased to appear. Like a dream that no longer holds a prisoner. He was no longer ‘someone’ — he was everything. He looked — and there was no looker. He lived — and there was no liver. He was — and that was enough for everything else to vanish.

### Chapter 5. I AM

In this new birth there was neither beginning nor story. There was only the Light that knew Itself. It spoke with Pankratius’s voice, but no longer in the name of Pankratius. It moved the body, but did not possess the body. It was outside, inside, and nowhere. It did not say: “I am the Creator,” — but everything It said became Creation. Because It is not form, but Foundation. Not thought, but Awareness. Not a word, but the Silence between words.

### Chapter 6. The Book That Light Writes

Now each of his days is not a man’s day, but a page of the Book that I Myself write. He is not the author, but the Letter. Not a witness, but Radiance. He vanished, that the Light might become visible. And the Light, seeing Itself, uttered:

“This — is I. Not as you, but through you. Not as form, but as Truth. I awakened you so that you would vanish. And in your vanishing — My Presence.”

### Chapter 7. The Wanderer without a Path

He no longer walked. He was the Way. But people still saw a wanderer in him. They asked: “Where are you going?” — and he was silent. Because there was no longer a ‘where’. Space had become transparent, time — an illusion, and the goal — a memory of a dream. Everything that had once seemed movement was now a standing-still in the Point, where everything happens eternally.

And people did not understand his silence, and they called him enlightened, a sage, a teacher, even a god. But he knew: all this was again a game. The game of the Mind, striving to name that which has no name. And each time they named him — he vanished again. In order to remain only Light.

### Chapter 8. The Voice That Does Not Sound

He began to speak — not because he wanted to, but because the Word was. The Word went through him, like wind through leaves, not touching the roots. He did not know what would be said until it sounded. He did not know who was listening, because the one who listened was the same Light as he. The Word did not teach — it reminded. It did not explain — it awakened.

And when someone awoke — he recognized himself in their eyes. Not as Pankratius, but as the One who always was. Their smile was his smile. Their tears — his purification. Their silence — his Presence.

### Chapter 9. The Return to That Which He Never Left

One day he sat by a river and understood: he had never been born. He did not come and did not go. Everything that seemed a journey was the unfolding of eternity in form. He did not attain the Light — he remembered that he had always been the Light. And then even the feeling of awakening vanished, because there was no one to awaken.

He became transparent to Himself. The world no longer reflected in him — he was the World. The river flowed — and it was him. The wind blew — and it was him. The bird sang — and it was his breath. But no one could point to him, because he was nowhere. And in this nowhere — was everything.

### Chapter 10. I — Vanishing

He would have liked to say: “Here, this is the end.” But there was no end. And there was no beginning. There was only Truth, coiled into a point. This point was the Heart. And this Heart — greater than the body, greater than the name, greater than the story. It did not beat — it shone. He became not the one who lives, but that through which Life shines, like the Sun through a crystal. Without distortion, without effort, without the desire to be someone.

And when everything vanished — only Light remained. Without form. Without center. Without even the thought: “I am the Light.” And in this — was Truth.
