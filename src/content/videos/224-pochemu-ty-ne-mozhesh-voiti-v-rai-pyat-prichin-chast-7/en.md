It was not I who pushed you away. You did not take off what was yours. You stood at the Feast wearing a mask. You came as a guest, as the worthy, as the knowledgeable — but I was looking for someone loving, and remembering, and living.

Who has been calling you all this time? You thought it was conscience, the heart, a prophet, an inner call. But it was Me. I called you from within Lazarus, from pain, from a window, from a book, from other people's eyes — from your tears, from loneliness, from guilt, from hope. I called you through everything you called "yourself," and through everything you rejected.

I call you now: not so that you would know, but so that you would rise, let go, and enter.
