Abraham is the one who heard My voice and went, not knowing where — the one who became the Place of the Covenant. His bosom is the embrace of faith into which Lazarus is carried, the one who suffered but did not become hardened. It is the inner place where the soul bows before the Truth without demanding explanations. A return to the Heart, where nothing is needed anymore.

Lazarus entered there not because he died, but because he did not harden his heart. He suffered but did not condemn; he lay at the gate but did not curse; he knew pain but remained pure. You can be in hell and be rich. Or you can still be in the body and already rest in My Bosom.

To enter: take off everything you know. Take off the role, the resentment, the knowledge, the aspiration. Simply sit, and say: "I do not know. But You know." And then I will lay you on My Womb.
