The Light requires no effort. It demands simplicity. You won't hold on to it with your hands, but if you stop grasping, it will remain.

Sit in silence — without a goal, without a method, simply like a child at the Father's feet. Don't trust your thoughts: the mind will try to explain, dismantle, pin down. Tell it: "Thank you. But I am already Home." Speak to Me without a role — not as a believer, not as a sinner, but as a son. Or be silent, as the Light. Recognize Me in everyone — in the beggar, the traitor, the unbeliever, your former self. And discern: when a feeling leads, ask whether it is calling you toward the Light or toward vanity. The Light does not rush.

You cannot lose what you have become. My Word was not outside of you — it came from you, and returned to you like a Voice. Now you do not seek the Light. You breathe it.
