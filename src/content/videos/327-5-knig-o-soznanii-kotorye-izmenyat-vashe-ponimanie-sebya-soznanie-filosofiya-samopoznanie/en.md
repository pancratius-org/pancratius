"The Gospel of the Kingdom" describes a world in which a person begins to recognize their own depth and responsibility for their life. It takes up the themes of the Bible and the words of Christ, but reads them as an invitation to inner awakening.

"Theological Mathematics: Aleph Beyond Aleph" combines philosophy, symbolic language, and an attempt to understand the absolute through reason — a reflection on the boundary between logic and the experience that many describe as a void filled with meaning and light.

"Personality and Ego" explores how the self-image is formed, why internal conflict arises, and how a person can move beyond the beliefs that confine them.

"Gaze" brings together the psychology of consciousness and the philosophy of perception around an unusual idea: the world is not simply perceived by humans, but is substantially shaped by how they look at it.

"Mother of Light" reflects on the image of the feminine principle through which light, love, and life are manifested in the world — not as religious cult, but as a meditation on the profound symbolism of motherhood, tenderness, and presence.
