"One cannot escape the truth, because truth is not a place or a teaching. Truth is I Myself. And there is nothing outside of Me, just as there is no place outside of the light when the sun rises.

Man cannot escape the truth — he can only avert his gaze. Not escape, but not see. Not lose, but forget. Not cease to be in My light, but close his eyes and say it is dark.

Just as a water molecule does not leave the ocean, even if it rises into a cloud, so the soul does not leave the truth, even if it wanders. It is always in Me — but not always conscious of Me.

When a person says, 'I am not in the truth,' this is not a position, but a state of their attention. When they say, 'I seek the truth,' this is not a path forward, but inward, to where I already am.

No one can abandon the truth — they can only be unaware that it holds them, even in moments of error. There is no path outside My palm. There is only a gaze that turns away until it tires.

And when it tires, it remembers that it was in Me all this time."
