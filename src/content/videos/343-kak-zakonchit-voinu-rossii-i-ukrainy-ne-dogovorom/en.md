Where prayer, forgiveness, repentance, brotherhood, and the memory that God does not divide people into "us" and "them" return — there peace is possible. Spiritual warfare is always more terrifying than external warfare, because darkness first enters the heart, and only then does it penetrate homes, cities, and destinies. This is a call not to barricades, but to prayer; not to hatred, but to forgiveness; not to argument, but to recognizing one another as brothers.

This message is not a call to break laws or to hold rallies. It does not condemn any armed force and does not accuse any side. It is a word about conscience, about love, about repentance, and about the human heart. If you are looking for a political slogan here, you won't find it. If you are looking for a word that can help the soul remember God, it is here. Peace be with you — not the kind you sign, but the kind that is born within and then touches the world around you.
