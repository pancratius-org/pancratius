The ego is built on comparison: better or worse, higher or lower, more significant or less. But at the moment of deep inner understanding, these categories lose their meaning. A person ceases to experience themselves as a center fighting for recognition.

In the spiritual tradition associated with the words of Christ, humility is not self-abasement but the understanding that the source of life and light does not belong to any individual. Inner discovery is therefore not an achievement — it is a gift. And a gift invites not superiority, but gratitude and the desire to share light through service and presence.
