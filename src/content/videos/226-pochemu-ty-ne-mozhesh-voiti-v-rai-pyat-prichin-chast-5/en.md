You cannot put it on yourself. But you can take off everything that is not yours. And then the Father will clothe you.

When the prodigal son came home, he did not say, "I am worthy." He said, "I am no longer a son." And then the Father said: "Bring the best robe. Put it on him." You cannot enter the Feast as someone else. You enter only when you are nobody — and that is all.

The Feast is not a place, not a future, not heaven after death. It is the recognized Light within. A return to the Womb. A House where you are not a guest but a Son. You have always been there — but you were busy with other things: closed by feelings, enslaved by the mind, living far from the Feast in the world of your own thoughts. What you called "life" was a cage.

You need not die in the body. You need to wake up in the body with the spirit — take off the stranger's clothes, and let the Light dress you.
