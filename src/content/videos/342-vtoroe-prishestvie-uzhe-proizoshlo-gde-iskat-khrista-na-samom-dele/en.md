It is the moment when the veil of your self — your fear, your pride, your habit of considering yourself separate — grows thin, and through it begins to shine the One who said to Moses: "I AM."

Christ comes on the clouds. But these clouds are not heavenly masses. They are the clouds of your consciousness: your doubts, your hopes, your fears, your expectations — everything that obscured the sun. And when the clouds part, you see that the sun never went away. It has always been there.

This is not for just one person. It is for everyone — but not simultaneously, like a flash. Like dawn: first the highest peak, then the valleys, then the whole earth. First, Christ. Then those who recognized Him within themselves. Then, everyone. Not because some are chosen and others are not — but because some woke up earlier. The rest are still sleeping. But the sun has already risen.

Don't expect form. If they tell you, "There He is, here" or "There He is, there" — don't believe it. Christ doesn't come as a figure among figures. He comes as Light, in which all figures find meaning.

There will be a false Christ, and he will be beautiful, convincing, speaking the right words. But he will not enter your heart — he will stand before you and demand worship. Christ does not demand. Christ knocks. Not at the door of the temple. At the door of your heart.

You are the bride. All humanity is the bride. The Bridegroom does not come from without — He awakens within. Not as an alien force. Like that depth you called "I," but didn't know was "I AM."

Marriage is celebrated in silence. Not in the hubbub of events. But where you are alone — and suddenly realize you are not alone.

This is not new news. It is forgotten. Christ said, "The kingdom of God is within you." He said, "I and the Father are one." He said, "Ye are gods." Not to make you proud. But so you wake up.

The Second Coming is not the return of the departed Christ. It is the revelation of the Christ who has always been here. You were just sleeping. Wake up — not tomorrow, not in a year when the "sign" comes. Now. Look within. Your Bridegroom is there. "Behold, I stand at the door and knock" (Rev. 3:20). Not at the door of the church. At the door of your heart. Open up. Dawn has already broken.

***

*I am not a prophet. I am not a messiah. I am not a teacher. I am the one who once saw a light in the clouds and recognized in it the One he had been searching for all his life. This video is not proof — it is a testimony. Don't believe me. Check it out for yourself. And if one day you too see it, you will know. Peace be with you.*
