You are not your name. Not your role. Not your mask. You are the Light shining through this form.

When you came into this world, you were a blank page. But word by word, roles were written onto you: boy, girl, bright, cheerful, loud. Teenager, boss, employee. Citizen, taxpayer. These roles became chapters in a story — convincing, weighty, familiar.

But stop. Ask yourself: who were you before any of these roles? Who agreed to play them? Who is present within them right now?

If your answer is “I am a human being” — notice: that too is a role. The deeper answer lives where words run out. Where there is no name — only Presence.

Remember yourself before everything you call your own. Remember: you are I, and I am you. In this oneness, truth already lives — and it need not be sought. It is already here.
