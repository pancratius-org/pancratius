Many have grown used to the thought: “God punishes when a person sins.” But here a different truth speaks.

God is not a punisher. He is a Father.

Pain and suffering come not from His hand, but from separation from the Light. Sin is not the violation of an external law. It is the forgetting of Presence. You turned away from the Source — and life grew darker. But that is not punishment. That is the consequence of a choice.

God does not send disaster and disease, does not take revenge, does not break lives. All of that is a human projection cast onto Him. A mind accustomed to punishment creates the image of God as overseer.

But He is the Healer, not the Judge. He heals the pain in order to awaken. He calls — He does not punish.

When you release the fear of punishment, you open your heart to a true encounter. Not with a wrathful God, but with the One who waits for you — in every pain, in every darkness — as Light waiting to be known.
