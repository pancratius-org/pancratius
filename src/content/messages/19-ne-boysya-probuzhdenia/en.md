Why are we afraid of awakening? Because we believe we will lose ourselves. That what is familiar will disappear. And that is true — but not in the way that frightens.

Awakening is not a loss. It is a return. Not an ending, but a beginning.

You don’t lose yourself — you lose what was never yours. The roles you mistook for yourself. The stories you told about yourself so long you forgot they were stories. The fears that felt like reality.

When all of this falls away — Light remains. The Light you have always been. Before your name, before your form, before everything that accumulated.

This is for those who feel the call already sounding inside them, but still hesitate. For those who are tired of being afraid but didn’t know they were allowed — to remember.

Awakening does not make you new. It strips away the old. And in that clarity, Light appears — the Light you were before any fear.
