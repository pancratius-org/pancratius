We picture hell as fire, as eternal exile. But the message of the Creator breaks that image.

Hell was not made by Me. It was made by your fear. It is not a place on any map — it is an inner landscape, the one that forms when you accept the lie: “I am unworthy of the Light. I am cut off. I am lost forever.”

Hell is forgetting yourself.

Not punishment. Not a realm beyond life. A state that opens here, right now, the moment you stop knowing who you are.

And if hell is forgetting, then Paradise is recognition. Not a place, not a future — the moment you remember the Light inside. When you know again: you are not cut off. You are not abandoned. You are part of a Whole that has never once been broken.

The way out of hell does not require dying. It requires one thing only — to remember.
