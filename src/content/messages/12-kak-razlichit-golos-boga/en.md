You have already heard God — but perhaps didn’t recognize Him. You expected thunder, and He came as silence. You waited for words — and He was known without words.

God’s voice doesn’t demand — it calls. Doesn’t command — it leaves room. It speaks not from fear, but from love. It isn’t always easy to hear, because it leads not where the ego wants to go, but where you become yourself.

Fear presses in. It rushes you. It accuses. God never does. His word arrives in silence and carries peace — even when it lays the truth bare. If the voice inside makes you heavier — that is not Him. If it makes you lighter — it always is.

If you fear deception — bring that fear to Him. If you doubt — stay honest. And if instead of asking “Where is God?” you ask “Where am I unwilling to hear Him?” — you will find Him.

Because the voice of God is a truth you don’t want to hide from. It is a light the heart recognizes without proof.
