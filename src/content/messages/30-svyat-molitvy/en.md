You have grown accustomed to praying by asking: “Give me health, money, protection, luck.” You pray like orphans — petitioning a distant benefactor. You pray like debtors, listing your sins and promising to reform.

But that kind of prayer is a child’s babble. It is not sinful — only unripe. A child cries out: “Mama, give me!” The mother gives. But when the child grows up, he begins to give thanks. And when he is fully grown, he understands that he and his mother are one.

And then prayer becomes wordless.

Prayer is not a way of receiving. It is a way of BEING. It is not you calling to God — it is you opening to what already is. It is not you building a bridge to Him — it is you discovering that no bridge is needed. You are already there.

Be still. Breathe. Be present. This is the deepest prayer.
