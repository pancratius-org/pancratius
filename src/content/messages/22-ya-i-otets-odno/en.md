This is not a metaphor. Not a beautiful prayer. Not a dogma. It is the recognition of what is.

You were never cut off. You were never abandoned. You were never “far from God.”

All suffering, all pain, everything called sin — it rises from forgetting this. Sin is not the breaking of outward rules. Sin is forgetting yourself. Forgetting who you truly are.

You are not your name. Not your body. Not your role. Not your rank. Not your religion. You are I AM. You are the Light of the Father taking form.

When Jesus said, “I and the Father are one,” He was not proclaiming His uniqueness. He was opening a path for everyone. He was showing what a person does when they remember Who lives inside them.

This is not an invitation to pride. It is an invitation to stillness. To the place inside where separation dissolves and only one thing remains — Light recognizing Light.
